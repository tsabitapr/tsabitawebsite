Surabaya

Surabaya, East Java's capital, thrives as a metropolis with a bustling seaport, rich history, diverse economy, and vibrant culture. Iconic landmarks like Heroes Monument and Suramadu Bridge reflect its heritage and modernity, while local cuisine adds flavor to its appeal for residents and visitors.

Surabaya is my hometown, and it has been my cherished abode since the day I was born. While one might expect that a lifetime spent in the same place could breed familiarity and perhaps even boredom, I find that my affection for this city only deepens with time. Its bustling streets, vibrant markets, and the enduring warmth of its people have etched an indelible mark in my heart. Despite the allure of distant horizons, I remain steadfast in my commitment to Surabaya, for it is here that my roots are firmly planted, and here I intend to stay until the wisdom of old age graces my years.

Local Food

Surabaya's local food scene is a gastronomic delight, a fusion of rich flavors and diverse culinary traditions. It offers a journey through the city's cultural tapestry, a feast for both the palate and the senses.

- Rujak Buah -

Spicy, sweet, and fruity Indonesian salad

- Pecel -

Surabaya's iconic spicy peanut salad, bursting with flavors.

- Rawon -

Javanese beef soup, dark from keluak nuts, intensely flavorful.

- Lontong Balap -

Surabayan dish; rice cake, tofu, bean sprouts, savory sauce.

- Tahu Campur -

A Surabayan dish with mixed tofu, bean sprouts, meat, and a savory broth, creating a harmonious blend of flavors and textures.

- Tahu Tek -

Surabayan specialty featuring fried tofu, crackers, boiled eggs, and a sweet-spicy peanut sauce, creating a delightful blend of textures and flavors.

- Rujak Cingur -

East Javanese salad with beef snout and vegetables

- Sego Sambel Mak Yeye -

Rice with spicy sambal sauce, famous for its bold and fiery flavors, often accompanied by various side dishes.

- Sate Klopo Ondomohen -

Delicacy of coconut-infused satay, rich, and flavorful, typically served with aromatic rice cakes and a tasty peanut sauce.

- Semanggi -

Surabayan dish, bean sprouts, crackers, clover leaf, and sweet-spicy peanut sauce, a flavorful and crunchy delight with a hint of heat.

- Gado Gado -

Indonesian salad, colorful mix of vegetables, tofu, tempeh, and peanut sauce, creating a delicious and satisfying medley of textures and flavors.

Tourist Places

Surabaya's tourist spots showcase a captivating blend of architectural wonders, cultural richness, and natural beauty. The city's charm lies in its ability to seamlessly blend history with modernity, creating a captivating tapestry for visitors to explore. Each site holds unique stories and experiences that invite travelers to immerse themselves in the city's vibrant culture and diverse landscapes. Whether it's the grandeur of monumental landmarks or the serenity of coastal parks, Surabaya offers a beautiful and enchanting journey for all who visit.

- Tugu Pahlawan -

Iconic Surabaya monument honoring national heroes.

- Surabaya Statue -

Monument depicting the city's fighting spirit.

- Sanggar Agung Temple -

Surabaya's prominent Chinese Buddhist temple.

- 4 Form Buddha Statue -

Four-faced Buddha sculpture in Surabaya.

- House of Sampoerna Museum -

Historic tobacco museum in Surabaya.

- Mangrove Botanical Garden -

Surabaya's coastal conservation area, rich biodiversity.

- Suramadu Bridge -

Iconic bridge linking Surabaya and Madura Island.

- Surabaya North Quay -

Waterfront area offering leisure and entertainment.

- Kenjeran Park -

Coastal park with beaches, temples, and family attractions.

- Jalesveva Jayamahe Monument -

Maritime monument symbolizing Surabaya's naval heritage.

- Tunjungan Street -

Surabaya's historic shopping and cultural thoroughfare.

- Surabaya's Alun Alun -

Central city square with cultural and recreational attractions.
